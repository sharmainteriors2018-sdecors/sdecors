Wallpaper & Panels

Wallpaper vs Wall Panels: A Specifier's Comparison

Published 8 July 2026 · Sharma Interiors · 5 min read

Wallpaper and wall panels solve overlapping problems, which is exactly why they get confused at the spec stage. Both dress a flat wall. Beyond that, they behave very differently, in cost, durability and the kind of visual impact they deliver.

What wallpaper does well

Wallpaper is the right call when the wall needs to carry a pattern, colour or brand story rather than a texture. It's the cheaper option per square foot, quick to install, and easy to change out when a space is refreshed on a shorter cycle: think hospitality rooms or retail concessions that get repositioned every few years.

What wall panels do well

Panels, whether fluted, WPC or 3D textured, earn their higher cost in high-traffic or high-visibility zones where a flat printed surface would show wear, or where the brief calls for a tactile, dimensional finish rather than a flat pattern. Lobbies, reception counters and corridors that get touched, brushed against or knocked take panels better than paper.

Durability and maintenance

  • Wallpaper: vulnerable to scuffing and moisture in high-traffic corridors; easy and cheap to reprint or replace.
  • Wall panels: resist knocks and scuffing far better; replacement is a bigger job if damaged, since panels are usually fixed with adhesive or clip systems.

Budgeting for a mix

Most fit-outs don't need to pick one exclusively. A common, budget-efficient split is: panels on the lower "impact zone" of a wall (reception desks, corridor dado height) and wallpaper above it or in lower-traffic rooms. This keeps the durable material where it's needed and saves cost everywhere else.

Treat the choice as a traffic-and-touch question first, and a look-and-feel question second. The visual result usually takes care of itself once the wall is doing the right job.
